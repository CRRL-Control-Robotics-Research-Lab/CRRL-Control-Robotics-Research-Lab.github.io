Robotics

We focus on creating safe and reliable autonomous systems capable of complex tasks in unstructured and dynamic environments. A core emphasis is on safety-critical control synthesis using Control Barrier Functions (CBFs) and Differentiable Optimization techniques to manage constraints in real time. We develop frameworks for Human-Robot Interaction (HRI) that proactively prioritize safety based on human pose and body detection, mitigating potential harm from unavoidable collisions. Our work extends to safe navigation for systems like quadruped robots using point-cloud based CBFs and developing lightweight localization methods to enhance real-time autonomy.

Systems & Control

Our Control Theory research develops the next generation of nonlinear control methods that offer explicit, rigorous performance guarantees, moving beyond asymptotic stability. The core problem addressed is achieving prescribed-time stabilization: ensuring a system's state converges to the origin within an arbitrary finite time interval specified by the control designer, regardless of the initial state. Our novel methodology relies on Dynamic High-Gain Scaling techniques combined with specialized time transformations and Lyapunov analysis to design robust controllers. Furthermore, we utilize Matrix Pencil-based approaches to algebraically formalize the problem, allowing us to precisely characterize the inherent design freedoms within the controller structure. This minimizes design conservatism and increases the feasibility and efficiency of implementing high-performance stabilizing control laws.

Cyber Physical Systems & Security

Our research focuses on the vulnerabilities and defense mechanisms within Cyber-Physical Systems (CPS), addressing threats from the software level down to the hardware. We develop novel techniques for vulnerability discovery in software by creating Interactive Agent Tools that enable Large Language Model (LLM) agents to run interactive utilities like debuggers for solving complex Capture The Flag (CTF) challenges. On the hardware and embedded system side, we pioneer golden-free detection methods for Hardware Trojans in PCBs and ICs by monitoring multimodal side channels and exploiting short-term aging effects. Other work explores tamper-proof network traffic measurements on the NIC for reliable intrusion detection.

Trustworthy and Resilient AI

This area is dedicated to providing formal, provable guarantees for the stability, safety, and resilience of complex AI and machine learning systems. We establish theoretical limits and develop practical methods for certified robustness in deep neural networks (DNNs), including defending against backdoor attacks and providing provably robust perceptual similarity metrics. A key focus is on applying formal verification techniques to complex systems like segmentation models using randomized smoothing and diffusion models. Furthermore, we develop switching and re-initialization strategies to enhance resilience of CPS against attacks, guaranteeing the mean-square boundedness of system states through formal analysis.
