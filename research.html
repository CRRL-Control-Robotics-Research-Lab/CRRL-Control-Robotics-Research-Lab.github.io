Robotics

We focus on creating safe and reliable autonomous systems capable of complex tasks in unstructured and dynamic environments. A core emphasis is on safety-critical control synthesis using Control Barrier Functions (CBFs) and Differentiable Optimization techniques to manage constraints in real time. We develop frameworks for Human-Robot Interaction (HRI) that proactively prioritize safety based on human pose and body detection, mitigating potential harm from unavoidable collisions. Our work extends to safe navigation for systems like quadruped robots using point-cloud based CBFs and developing lightweight localization methods to enhance real-time autonomy.

Systems & Control

Our research develops advanced control methods for a broad spectrum of systems, spanning areas such as multi-agent networks, nonlinear and resilient control, and decentralized large-scale architectures. By bridging rigorous theoretical analysis with practical applications—including safe and learning-based control—we aim to solve fundamental challenges in the coordination of next-generation dynamic systems.

Cyber Physical Systems & Security

Our research focuses on advancing cybersecurity for both Cyber-Physical Systems (CPS) and AI-driven security automation. In CPS security, we develop resilient defensive methods by improving static and dynamic binary analysis, enhancing real-time operational monitoring, and designing anomaly detection techniques that leverage side channels such as hardware performance counters and power consumption. Complementing this, we advance AI for cybersecurity by training transformer models for reverse engineering, building LLM-powered agents for autonomous offensive and defensive tasks, and creating benchmarks that evaluate their performance on complex security challenges. Together, these efforts push the boundaries of system visibility, intelligent analysis, and automated threat response.

Trustworthy and Resilient AI

This area is dedicated to providing formal, provable guarantees for the stability, safety, and resilience of complex AI and machine learning systems. We establish theoretical limits and develop practical methods for certified robustness in deep neural networks (DNNs), including defending against backdoor attacks and providing provably robust perceptual similarity metrics. A key focus is on applying formal verification techniques to complex systems like segmentation models using randomized smoothing and diffusion models. Furthermore, we develop switching and re-initialization strategies to enhance resilience of CPS against attacks, guaranteeing the mean-square boundedness of system states through formal analysis.
